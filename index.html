About Me

Hello! My name is Zainab Memon and I'm from Champaign, Illinois. I am a junior in Computer Engineering with a minor in Mathematics and am interested in specializing in cybersecurity. I enrolled in the Leadership Certificate Program in the spring of my sophomore year because I'm passionate about developing effective leadership skills to drive positive change in any situation. I believe in empowering teams and fostering environments where everyone can thrive, and I see leadership as the crucial catalyst for making that happen.

For me, leadership isn't just about giving directions or being in charge. It's about inspiring a shared vision and empowering others to contribute their unique strengths. I've always been drawn to the idea of bringing people together, whether it's through collaborative projects or community initiatives. I've seen firsthand how a supportive leader can transform a group of individuals into a cohesive, high-performing team, capable of achieving far more than they could alone. This desire to uplift and enable others is what truly drives my interest in leadership. It's about building strong relationships, fostering an inclusive atmosphere, and ultimately, creating a ripple effect of positive impact.

Personal Development Plan

Guided by reflection and intention, my development plan outlines the leadership goals I set for myself as part of this program. It captures my values, growth priorities, and the strategies I’m using to become a more thoughtful and effective leader.

Timeline

    The leadership course I took was ENG377: Pedagogy & Mentoring for ELAs (Engineering Learning Assistants), taught by Kimberely Powers. This course served as preparation for my Fall 2025 role leading a section of ENG100 for freshmen, where I will introduce them to Grainger College of Engineering and the ECE Department.

  • Spring 2025 Semester - Present: Co-Curricular Team Experience

    Through my role as Publicity Team Chair for the Electrical and Computer Engineering Student Advancement Committee, I’ve learned that leadership is not about directing every move but fostering collaboration and creating an environment where team members feel heard and empowered. I was responsible for overseeing tasks like designing posts for Instagram, advertising on the TVs in the ECE building, and working on the committee’s new website. These projects required careful coordination with other subcommittees and individual team members to ensure alignment with our goals and deadlines.

    I learned that leadership often involves balancing multiple perspectives, considering how to engage the broader ECE student body, and encouraging team members to bring their ideas forward. Rather than simply making decisions myself, I worked to guide the team toward solutions by facilitating discussions and providing support when needed.

    One of the most challenging aspects of my role was managing the creation of our new website, which involved shifting relevant content from the old website while ensuring that the new site aligned with the committee’s goals and vision. The transition presented several obstacles, including understanding the technical details of website design and managing the coordination of team members responsible for different sections. At the same time, there was pressure to ensure that the old content didn’t get lost, so I had to stay on top of every update and maintain a clear communication loop within the team.

    I navigated this challenge by staying organized and breaking the tasks into smaller, manageable steps. I created a project timeline and delegated specific content migration tasks to team members based on their strengths, while I focused on ensuring that the overall look and feel of the website aligned with our branding. By being clear in communication and flexible with deadlines, we were able to successfully launch the new site. This experience taught me that leadership requires patience and the ability to adapt when things don’t go as planned.

  • January 30, 2025: Clifton Strengths Workshop

    The CliftonStrengths workshop gave me valuable insights into my natural talents and how I can leverage them in my academic, professional, and personal life. Learning that my top strengths are Harmony, Relator, Empathy, Deliberative, and Consistency helped me understand why I prioritize collaboration, deep connections, and thoughtful decision-making.

    One key takeaway was recognizing how my strengths shape my leadership style. Harmony and Relator make me adept at fostering inclusive environments, ensuring everyone feels heard and valued. Empathy enhances my ability to support others, while Deliberative and Consistency enable me to make careful, fair decisions. These qualities will be especially useful as I take on leadership roles, such as in the ECE Student Advancement Committee, Illinois Student Council, and beyond, where I’ll need to balance different perspectives and execute plans effectively.

    Moving forward, I will focus on refining these strengths to become a more intentional leader and team member. By embracing my natural tendencies while also pushing myself to take risks, I can create meaningful connections, foster collaboration, and ensure that my contributions have a lasting impact.

  • March 12, 2025: Leading with Empathy and Compassion Workshop

    The workshop deepened my understanding of the nuances between pity, sympathy, empathy, and compassion. I learned that while pity and sympathy create distance, true empathy fosters connection by allowing us to understand and share another’s emotions. Compassion goes a step further, combining empathy with action. This distinction reinforced the importance of not just feeling for others but actively supporting them.

    Additionally, I learned that while active listening focuses on comprehension and response, empathetic listening prioritizes understanding emotions and validating experiences. This skill is crucial in leadership, as it builds trust and meaningful relationships. I will apply these insights in my leadership roles by practicing empathetic listening with my peers and taking proactive steps to support those in need by creating an environment where people feel heard and valued.

  • April 14, 2025: Leading With Integrity Leadership Lab

    This program has been a meaningful experience that challenged me to think deeply about ethics, integrity, and the kind of leader I want to be. I appreciated the opportunity to reflect on real-life dilemmas and learn practical frameworks for making values-based decisions. Overall, it gave me tools and insights that I’ll carry forward in both personal and leadership settings.

  • April 16, 2025: Strengths Based Leadership Lab

    My experience in the Strengths Based Leadership Lab was incredibly insightful and empowering. I learned how to identify and apply my unique strengths in team settings, which helped me better understand my leadership style and how to collaborate effectively with others. The interactive activities and reflections challenged me to grow both personally and professionally.

  • Summer 2025: Began developing this ePortfolio to reflect on and share my Leadership Certificate journey.

    Creating this was an incredible opportunity to combine my technical skills with personal reflection. It allowed me to thoughtfully document my growth, synthesize key takeaways from the program, and present my leadership development in a way that feels both meaningful and authentic.

  • September 21, 2025 Creating a Culture of Feedback Workshop

    The Creating a Culture of Feedback workshop made me think about how I give and receive feedback. In the past, I’ve sometimes felt defensive when getting feedback or worried about how I might sound when giving it to others. This workshop helped me see that feedback doesn’t have to be uncomfortable. It can actually build stronger relationships if it’s given with empathy and received with openness. Practicing the feedback model gave me tools I can use to make conversations feel less tense and more productive. Moving forward, I want to create spaces where people feel safe sharing honest thoughts, whether that’s in group projects, leadership roles, or future professional teams. I hope to approach feedback not as criticism, but as a chance to grow and support others in their growth too.

  • September 24, 2025 Navigating Multicultural Organizations Workshop

    The Navigating Multicultural Organizations workshop was an eye-opening experience because it pushed me to reflect on how my Pakistani background and American identity intertwine in ways I hadn’t always considered. I realized that I don’t fully fit into either cultural “norm.” For example, I identify more with American linear-time values, where being punctual and following strict schedules feels natural to me, rather than the more flexible approach to time common in Pakistani culture. At the same time, I connect deeply with the high-context communication style I grew up with in my Pakistani family, which contrasts with the low-context, direct style that dominates American spaces. Recognizing these differences showed me that I carry unique strengths from both cultures. Moving forward, I want to use this awareness to better navigate multicultural teams, valuing different perspectives while also being conscious of the assumptions I bring to interactions.

Reflection

Learning about my strengths helped me recognize my natural inclination toward thoughtful collaboration and emotional awareness. The empathy workshop expanded this by teaching me to actively listen and respond with compassion, not just understanding. Together, these experiences have shaped my leadership style, which is grounded in connection, fairness, and intentional action.

The Creating a Culture of Feedback workshop built on these lessons by helping me view feedback not as something to fear, but as a tool for mutual growth. I learned that when given and received with empathy, feedback can strengthen relationships and build trust. Meanwhile, the Navigating Multicultural Organizations workshop encouraged me to reflect on how my Pakistani and American identities influence how I communicate and collaborate. Recognizing the strengths of both cultural perspectives has made me more aware of my assumptions and more open to different ways of thinking and leading.

Moving forward, I will apply these insights by fostering inclusive, respectful environments where people feel heard, valued, and supported. Whether in student leadership roles or future professional settings, I hope to lead with empathy, cultural awareness, and a commitment to building meaningful, values-driven impact.

Leadership Competencies

Self-Management

Through the program, I developed stronger self-awareness and strategies for managing my time, energy, and emotions. I learned to set clear goals, prioritize tasks, and stay grounded under pressure. Reflection activities helped me identify patterns in my behavior and adjust my habits to align better with my values and responsibilities. I became more intentional about how I approach challenges and how I hold myself accountable. Building resilience, setting boundaries, and practicing mindfulness all contributed to my growth. I now feel more in control of my actions and better equipped to navigate stress, maintain focus, and lead with consistency and purpose.

Openness

I have become more open to feedback as a tool for growth rather than criticism. Instead of reacting defensively, I now listen to understand and reflect on how I can improve. Receiving feedback, especially the difficult kind, has helped me recognize blind spots, challenge assumptions, and strengthen my skills. I’ve learned to welcome different perspectives, ask clarifying questions, and apply insights constructively. This mindset shift has made me more adaptable, humble, and self-aware. By embracing feedback as a continuous learning opportunity, I have grown not only in confidence but also in my ability to lead with empathy and a willingness to evolve.

Communication

The program helped me develop more intentional, clear, and empathetic communication habits. Whether participating in workshops or team discussions, I practiced articulating my ideas more concisely and listening actively to others. The focus on empathetic listening in particular improved how I respond to and validate different viewpoints. I also became more mindful of nonverbal communication and the importance of aligning words with intent. These experiences have strengthened my ability to build trust, resolve conflicts, and foster open dialogue in group settings. Overall, I have become more confident in my communication and better equipped to lead with clarity and connection.

Group Dynamics

Participating in group-based workshops and leadership activities gave me insight into how effective group dynamics shape collaboration. I learned how to observe and respond to different team roles, mediate conflicting perspectives, and align team efforts toward shared goals. By fostering inclusive environments where all members felt valued, I improved my ability to facilitate collaboration and manage challenges with sensitivity and structure. These experiences taught me that strong group dynamics require trust, clear roles, and mutual accountability. I now approach team settings with the mindset of a facilitator, aiming to empower others while ensuring cohesion, productivity, and shared responsibility.

Overall Experience

The Leadership Certificate experience has been a steady process of discovery. It pushed me to reflect, adapt, and grow. Early on, I expected to learn techniques or best practices. Instead, I found myself returning to the fundamentals: who I am, how I show up, and what kind of impact I want to have on others. The program became a space to question assumptions I held about leadership, including the idea that it is something you are granted through a title or role. I began to see leadership as something you cultivate day by day, moment by moment, through your presence, decisions, and relationships.

One of the most meaningful parts of the experience was receiving feedback, both formally and informally. I realized how rarely we give each other honest, constructive insights. At first, I felt defensive. But over time, I grew to appreciate how feedback can be a gift, allowing me to try again and improve. It helped me notice habits I had not questioned and guided me toward better alignment between my intentions and impact. A challenge I faced was figuring out how to apply what I was learning in real time, especially when stakes felt high or uncertain. But even in moments of failure or conflict, I could feel the skills taking root. I started pausing before reacting, checking in with others, and staying open to dialogue. Small practices like active listening or naming assumptions began to shift the way I operated in group settings. I learned to value not just results, but relationships; not just outcomes, but how we get there.

Looking back, I think of this journey as assembling a puzzle. Each conversation, reflection, or challenge added a piece. Some pieces were uncomfortable, like confronting my fear of conflict or my tendency to over-function in teams, but they were necessary to understand the bigger picture. The puzzle remains a work in progress. Not because it’s unfinished in a frustrating way, but because leadership is something I will continue piecing together and growing throughout my life. This program helped me find the corner pieces: the core values, practices, and questions that will guide me no matter where I go. From here, I feel equipped to keep building with intention, humility, and a clearer sense of purpose.
